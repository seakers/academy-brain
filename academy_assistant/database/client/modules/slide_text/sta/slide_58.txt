Emissivity and absorptivity of materials

\[
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
\text{Material} & \alpha_{VNIR} & \epsilon_{TIR} & \text{Ratio} \\
\hline
\text{Aluminum} & 0.09 & 0.03 & 3.00 \\
\text{White paint} & 0.20 & 0.92 & 0.22 \\
\text{Black paint} & 0.92 & 0.89 & 1.03 \\
\text{Silver Teflon} & 0.08 & 0.8 & 0.10 \\
\text{Aluminized Kapton} & 0.38 & 0.67 & 0.56 \\
\hline
\end{array}
\]

\[
\alpha_{VNIR}/\epsilon_{TIR}
\]

- Use Silver Teflon to minimize solar absorption and max emissions (e.g., for telescope mirrors)
- Use black paint to maximize energy transfer, both absorption and emission (e.g., interior of spacecraft)
- Use metals (e.g., Aluminum) to minimize both absorption and emission (e.g., instrument sun shield)
- Combine these surfaces to get almost any \(\alpha_{VNIR}/\epsilon_{TIR}\) you want

Effect of solar radiation, outgassing etc. is deposit of particles on surfaces that increase \(\alpha_{VNIR}\). Almost no effect on \(\epsilon_{TIR}\).
