Heat transfer mechanisms

• Conduction
  • Requires physical contact
  • Heat flows from high T to low T according to Fourier's law:
    \[
    \dot{Q} = \frac{\partial E}{\partial t} = k \cdot A \cdot \frac{\partial T}{\partial x}
    \]
  • Where \( k \) is the thermal conductivity \(\left[ \frac{W}{m \cdot K} \right]\)

• Convection
  • "Heat rises", requires gravity
    \[
    \dot{Q} = \frac{\partial E}{\partial t} = h \cdot A \cdot (T_{\text{object}} - T_{\text{fluid}})
    \]
  • \( h \) is the convective heat transfer coefficient \(\left[ \frac{W}{m^2 \cdot K} \right]\)
  • Usually not a factor in space

• Radiation
  • Through EM fields
    \[
    \dot{Q} = \sigma E A T^4
    \]
  • Dominates heat transfer in space.
