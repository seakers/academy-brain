Module: Satellite Remote Sensing Payloads
Slide Number: 97
Slide text: 
- Let us consider the case of N antennas separated by d and emitting \( a_n e^{i \phi_n} \). The total field from a direction \( \theta \) is proportional to
\[ E(\theta) \sim \sum_n a_n e^{i \phi_n - i n k d \sin \theta} \]
- Assuming equal amplitudes and phases \( a_n e^{i \phi_n} = a e^{i \phi} \), we obtain
\[ E(\theta) \sim a e^{i \phi} \sum_n e^{-i n k d \sin \theta} \]
- This sum is strongly dependent on the value of \( \psi = k d \sin \theta = \frac{2 \pi d}{\lambda} \sin \theta \).
