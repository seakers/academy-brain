Microwave radiometers – Spatial resolution

• Most MW radiometers use rectangular aperture antennas ➔ angular resolution is Δθ_i = λ/D_i (1.22λ/D for circular)
• Spatial resolution of a nadir-looking radiometer (rectangular aperture D) at altitude h:
  \[
  \tan \left( \frac{\Delta \theta}{2} \right) = \tan \left( \frac{\lambda}{2D} \right) \approx \frac{\lambda}{2D}; \quad \Delta x \approx \frac{\lambda h}{2D}; \quad \Delta x \approx \frac{h \lambda}{D}
  \]
• If rectangular antenna, then it is different for cross-track vs along-track
• If off-nadir angle of η_0, then:
  \[
  \Delta x = h \left[ \tan \left( \eta_0 + \frac{\Delta \theta}{2} \right) - \tan \left( \eta_0 - \frac{\Delta \theta}{2} \right) \right]
  \]
  \[
  \approx \frac{h \lambda}{D \cos^2 \eta_0}
  \]
